# SHATTERED LIGHT
## A Tabletop Roleplaying Game of Gems, Rebellion, and Connection

*Steven Universe and all related characters and concepts are the property of Rebecca Sugar and Cartoon Network. Shattered Light is an unofficial fan work created for personal, non-commercial use and is not affiliated with or endorsed by Rebecca Sugar or Cartoon Network.*

---

*In a universe where Pink Diamond never went to Earth, the empire never had a reason to question itself. Thousands of years of unchecked order have produced something that looks, from inside, almost peaceful. Every gem has a purpose. Every purpose has a place. The machinery is very quiet now.*

*Pink Diamond is no longer the youngest, uncertain, eager-to-please gem she once was. Millennia of rule have refined her original impulse — but what do they want? — into something controlling and absolute. She doesn't ask what gems want anymore. She decides. She has become convinced that she understands other gems better than they understand themselves, that her care is the only reliable care, and that any gem who claims to want something different simply hasn't been properly understood yet.*

*She is not cruel in the way Yellow Diamond is cruel. She is certain in a way that leaves no room for anyone else to be certain about anything.*

*Somewhere in the outer reaches of the empire, a rebellion is asking the question Pink Diamond stopped asking a long time ago.*

*What do you want?*

---

# PART ONE: HOW TO PLAY

---

## What This Game Is About

Shattered Light is a tabletop roleplaying game for three to six players, one of whom is the GM (Game Moderator). You play gems — magical beings of hard light and mineral, members of an interstellar empire — who have broken from the Diamond Authority and joined a rebellion.

The game is about connections. Fusion, trust, grief, chosen family, and the slow difficult work of figuring out who you are when someone has spent your entire existence telling you. Fighting is real and sometimes necessary, but it is never the point. The gems who change things in this fiction are the ones who understand other gems well enough to reach them.

Combat and conflict are secondary systems. Bonds are the primary resource. Everything flows from relationship.

---

## The Basics

When your gem attempts something uncertain and consequential, you roll dice. You build a pool of six-sided dice based on your relevant stat, add bonus dice from any active Bonds, roll the pool, and read the single highest result.

**6** — Clean success. You get what you want.
**4–5** — Success with a cost or complication. You get it, but something else happens.
**1–3** — Miss. The GM makes a move. Things get harder or more interesting.

Everything else in the rules is built on top of this foundation.

---

## The GM's Role

The GM plays the world — NPCs, factions, the empire, its agents, its consequences. They don't play to win. They play to make the fiction interesting, to complicate things in ways that feel true, and to make the players' choices matter.

On a miss, the GM makes a move. A GM move is never "nothing happens." Something shifts — an enemy acts, a truth surfaces, a window closes. The fiction moves forward, just not in the players' favor.

The GM also holds certain information the players don't: the subtle cracks in Amazonite's constituent relationship, the open question on each Rebel's sheet, the true agenda of the Assessor. This information surfaces through play, not exposition.

# PART TWO: STATS AND BONDS

---

## The Five Stats

Every gem has five stats, each rated 0–5. Stats measure capability across domains that matter in this fiction. They are not fantasy archetypes — gems don't have muscles. Their capabilities emerge from will, form, and relationship.

**FORM**
Your physical presence and capability as a gem. Weapon summoning, combat, endurance, imposing yourself on the material world. This isn't strength in a human sense — it's how completely your projected form does what you will it to do. A cracked gem rolls Form poorly not because they're weak but because they're fragmented.

**CLARITY**
Perception, insight, reading situations and people. Noticing what's hidden, understanding what someone actually means versus what they say, strategic thinking. Also covers gem-sense — perceiving other gems' emotional states, recognizing fusion signatures, detecting corruption. The stat for gems who observe before they act.

**RESONANCE**
Your capacity for genuine connection. Persuasion, empathy, trust-building, emotional honesty. This is not manipulation. A gem with high Resonance is genuinely open, not calculating. That's what makes it powerful and what makes it costly — you can't fake your way through a Resonance roll. The primary stat for talking an enemy down, earning trust, or holding a difficult conversation.

**RADIANCE**
Expression of self. Your gem's unique power, shapeshifting, creative solutions, doing things no one expects from a gem like you. This is the break-the-mold stat — it reflects how far you've grown beyond your assigned function. Also the stat for resisting erasure: attempts to reduce you back to what Homeworld said you were. Homeworld actively suppresses Radiance in gems below a certain caste. A gem raised entirely under the Authority may start with Radiance deliberately low — growing it is a character arc.

**RESOLVE**
Willpower, self-possession, the capacity to stay yourself under pressure. Resisting Diamond influence, maintaining form when things get bad, not shattering emotionally when someone cuts deep. Resolve doesn't work alone against Diamond Authority pressure — when you roll Resolve in those situations, your Bond ratings with your allies count as bonus dice. A gem who is isolated is genuinely more vulnerable to the empire. A gem surrounded by deep bonds is structurally harder to break. The math reflects the fiction.

---

## The Unified Scale

All stats, Bonds, and Harmony run on the same 0–5 scale. A number always means the same thing regardless of what it's measuring.

**0** — Absent, conditioned out, or severed. Stats at 0 represent active suppression — something Homeworld took from you, or a wound deep enough that the capability isn't there yet. Bonds at 0 mean either the relationship hasn't formed or something broke it. Both are story.

**1** — Emerging, fragile, present but unreliable. Nascent connections. The beginning of something.

**2** — Functional and genuine. Real capability. A real relationship. Stable but not deep.

**3** — Strong. A clear asset. Deep history. This gem and this relationship carry weight.

**4** — Defining. A core strength. Foundational — this relationship is part of who you are.

**5** — Mastery. Complete. Fully realized. Rare, earned slowly, significant every time it comes up.

---

## Building Your Pool

Your dice pool starts with dice equal to your relevant stat. Then add bonus dice from any Bond that is genuinely active in this moment — not just present, but meaningfully shaping what you're doing.

You can only add bonus dice from one Bond per roll. In the moment, you reach for whoever matters most right now. That choice is itself a small story beat.

Bond ratings run 1–4 (Bond 5 is the ceiling, but at 5 you add four dice — the maximum). A maxed Bond with a high stat means rolling nine or ten dice. That's rare and should feel rare — a gem and their closest ally operating at full trust in a moment that calls on everything they have together.

---

## Reading the Roll

Take the single highest die. Everything else is context.

**6** — Clean success. You get what you want.
**4–5** — Success with a cost or complication.
**1–3** — Miss. The GM makes a move.

The cost on a 4–5 is always chosen from the fiction. The GM picks one: you get what you wanted but it costs time you didn't have, or it costs something personal, or it works but someone sees you do it, or it works but it changes you slightly. The cost should feel like a consequence of this specific action in this specific scene, not a generic tax.

---

## The Second Die — Collateral

After reading your highest die, look at your second highest (if you rolled more than one). This die doesn't change your outcome but the GM can invoke it to add texture.

**Second die 5–6:** Something good happens alongside your main result — an unexpected opening, a moment of grace, an NPC reacting warmly.

**Second die 3–4:** Neutral ripple. Something else shifts in the scene but it's neither clearly good nor bad yet.

**Second die 1–2:** Something you didn't intend happened alongside your action. Not a failure — just a crack in the moment. A tell. A cost that might matter later.

The GM doesn't have to invoke the second die every roll. It's a tool for scenes that need texture, not a mandatory extra complication. It matters most on successes, where it adds richness without undercutting the win.

---

## Resonance — Multiple 6s

When your pool produces two or more 6s, something exceptional happens. This is called a Resonance result. It isn't just a bigger success — it's a qualitative shift. The GM and rolling player collaborate on what it means. It should feel like a moment the table remembers.

**Form Resonance** — your weapon does something it has never done before. A new form, a new reach, a capability that surprises even you. Or you take a hit that should have cracked you and it doesn't, and everyone felt something in that.

**Clarity Resonance** — you understand something completely. Not the surface read but the whole architecture. The GM tells you one true thing about the scene they weren't planning to reveal yet.

**Resonance Resonance** — genuine connection fires both ways. The NPC or enemy you were trying to reach actually opens. Not just compliance — real contact. This can permanently shift an NPC's relationship with the party.

**Radiance Resonance** — you do something nobody thought was possible for a gem like you. Mark it on your sheet. It permanently expands what your gem is capable of, and the GM acknowledges it in the fiction going forward.

**Resolve Resonance** — you don't just resist the pressure. You push back. Whatever was trying to erase or diminish you comes away changed by the contact. Against a Diamond Authority figure, they become briefly uncertain — a crack in their conviction, not yours.

---

## Bond Ratings

Bonds run 0–5 between any two gems. The number isn't just mechanical weight — it's a description of where the relationship lives right now.

**0 — Absent.** No real connection, or one that's been severed. You know each other exists.

**1 — Nascent.** You've chosen to trust this gem, at least a little. Something is there — curiosity, cautious respect, a moment that mattered. Fragile.

**2 — Real.** You know each other. You've been through something together. This gem's wellbeing registers with you.

**3 — Deep.** You've seen each other at your worst and stayed. There's history here. You think about this gem when they're not in the room.

**4 — Foundational.** This relationship is part of who you are now. Losing it would change you. You'd make hard choices for this gem.

**5 — Resonant.** Complete trust. Not blind — you know exactly who this gem is, including the hard parts, and you're here anyway. Rare. Earned slowly.

---

## Bond Asymmetry

Bonds are tracked per gem, not between gems. Your Bond with another gem is yours. Theirs with you is theirs. They can be different numbers, and that asymmetry is interesting.

You trust them at 4. They trust you at 2. That gap is a scene waiting to happen.

The GM can see both numbers. Players only see their own — unless they ask, and asking is itself a moment.

---

## How Stats and Bonds Grow

Both advance through marked moments, not experience points. When something happens in the fiction that genuinely tests or expresses a stat or relationship, you mark it. At the end of a session, marked stats and Bonds are eligible to advance by one step if the table agrees the moment warranted it.

You can only advance one stat and one Bond per session. Scarcity keeps growth meaningful.

Stats and Bonds can also decrease — a cracked gem loses a point of Form temporarily, a betrayal can drop a Bond immediately. The numbers breathe in both directions.

# PART THREE: FUSION

---

## What Fusion Is

Fusion is the act of two gems combining into a single entity — a third being that emerges from the relationship and couldn't have existed any other way. In this game, when two player characters fuse, both players share a single character. Getting this right is the most mechanically unique thing this system does.

A fusion isn't Player A with Player B's stats bolted on. It's a third entity with its own personality, its own capabilities, and its own sheet. The two players should feel like they're sharing something, not competing over it.

Fusion can't be forced. If one player doesn't want to fuse, it doesn't happen. No mechanic overrides this.

---

## Forming a Fusion

Fusion requires intent and resonance. Both players declare they want to fuse, then make a Bond roll using their current Bond rating with each other.

**Clean success (6):** The fusion forms fully. Begin at full Harmony.
**Partial success (4–5):** The fusion forms but starts with reduced Harmony — begin at 3 instead of 5.
**Miss (1–3):** The fusion doesn't form. Something is unresolved between them. This is a story beat, not just a failed roll.

---

## The Fusion Sheet

Each fusion between two specific gems has its own pre-made sheet, created collaboratively during session zero or the first time those two gems fuse. It is never generated on the fly.

**Name and form.** Invented together by the players. The fusion's appearance should blend both gems but feel like a distinct person — not a costume.

**Three unique stats** — not on either gem's individual sheet. These emerge from the relationship, not the gems' base natures. The players and GM define what each stat covers during session zero. They run on the same 0–5 scale as everything else.

**A Harmony track** — five boxes, starting full when fused cleanly. This is the fusion's structural integrity. It is not hit points. It represents how together the two gems are inside the fusion right now.

**Two signature moves** — things only this fusion can do. One physical, one relational. These should feel impossible for either gem alone.

---

## Session Zero — Building Every Fusion in Advance

Every possible PC-to-PC fusion should be created during session zero, before play begins. This is not optional bookkeeping — it is one of the most important things the group does together before the first session.

A party of three has three possible fusions. A party of four has six. A party of five has ten. Not all of them will happen in play. Some sheets may sit untouched for an entire campaign. But having them ready means a fusion can happen in the moment, dramatically, without pause — and the work of creating them is itself meaningful.

Two players sitting down and asking "who are we when we're together" is the same work the fusion does in the fiction. The conversation that produces a fusion's name, form, and stats is establishing the relationship before it's been tested. Players who have done this work know something about their characters' connection that hasn't been played yet. That knowledge shapes how they play those characters from session one onward.

---

### The Session Zero Fusion Process

For each possible pair of player characters, the two players work through the following together. The GM is present and listening — they contribute to the approval steps and may ask questions, but the fusion belongs to the two players.

**Step one: Name and form.** What is this fusion called? What do they look like? The appearance should blend both gems visually but feel like a distinct person — not a costume, not a combination. Something new emerged. The name should not obviously map to either constituent gem. Let the relationship determine the result, not arithmetic.

**Step two: Three unique stats.** What capabilities does this relationship produce that neither gem has alone? Name them and define what they cover. The names should say something true about the nature of the relationship. A fusion built on years of careful trust might have stats like Steadiness, Depth, and Precision. A fusion built on the collision of two very different gems might have Momentum, Surprise, and Weight. Stats run on the standard 0–5 scale. Assign starting values collaboratively — the GM approves the spread.

**Step three: Two signature moves.** One physical, one relational. These should feel impossible for either gem alone — not just a combination of their individual capabilities but something genuinely new. The same template applies as individual signature moves: When [specific circumstance], I can [effect]. Both moves require GM approval.

**Step four: A relationship note.** Write one sentence describing the quality of this specific fusion — what it feels like to be them, what the two gems are like together that they aren't separately. This isn't mechanical. It's for the table.

*"When they're Chrysocolla, the grief they both carry individually becomes something they can actually set down for a while."*

*"Vesuvianite is the version of both of them that has already decided — whatever doubt either of them carries alone, she doesn't have it."*

*"They become Pietersite when they're afraid, and Pietersite is not afraid of anything."*

---

### Fusions That Haven't Happened Yet

A fusion sheet created in session zero represents potential, not history. The sheet exists before the relationship has been tested enough for the fusion to occur naturally.

The first time two gems fuse in play is still a story moment, regardless of whether the sheet exists. The sheet is ready. Whether the gems are ready is a different question.

If a fusion's sheet was created in session zero but the constituent gems' Bond is still low, the first fusion attempt carries real risk of a miss or partial success — and that's right. The sheet describes who they could be together. Getting there is the arc.

---

### When an Unexpected Fusion Comes Up

Sometimes a fusion will happen that nobody prepared for — a gem type brought in mid-campaign, a fusion combination nobody thought would ever come up, a spontaneous moment that the story demanded before the table was ready for it.

This is fine. Pause. Take the time. Work through the session zero fusion process together at the table before play continues. This isn't an interruption — it's the game doing what it's supposed to do. A fusion that nobody saw coming is usually the most interesting one, and it deserves the same care as the ones you planned for.

The other players can take a break, talk through their own characters, or simply wait. No fusion sheet should be improvised under time pressure. The ten or fifteen minutes it takes to do it properly are always worth it.

---

## Playing the Fusion — The Echo Structure

When the fusion is active, both players share the character using the Echo structure.

One player is the Lead at any given moment. The Lead speaks first — initiating actions, dialogue, decisions. The other player is the Echo — completing, extending, deepening what the Lead said. Never redirecting, never contradicting. Finishing each other's sentences.

*"We reach out our hand—"*
*"—and we don't summon a weapon. We just hold it open."*

The Lead role passes freely between players, any time, with no roll required, as long as both are in agreement. Either player can signal they want the Lead by starting to speak — the other yields, or the Echo becomes the new Lead. This should feel like jazz, not a relay race.

The GM addresses the fusion as a single entity. They don't ask "what does Player A want" — they ask "what do you do?" Both players answer together.

---

## Harmony Checks

Roll a Harmony check when any of the following happen:

**Narrative disagreement.** If the two players genuinely can't agree on what the fusion does next — not as a bit, but as an actual conflict — the GM calls for a Harmony check. This is the most important trigger.

**Significant damage.** A hard physical hit that is narratively significant enough to shake the fusion's sense of self.

**Bond drop.** If something mid-scene damages the Bond between the two constituent gems — a revealed secret, a moment of blame, a fear surfacing — Harmony takes the hit immediately.

**Targeted pressure.** An enemy who understands fusion can attack the relationship rather than the body. This is a social or psychological attack and triggers a Harmony check instead of a damage roll.

On a Harmony check failure: lose one Harmony box. At two boxes remaining, the fusion's signature moves become unavailable. At one box, the GM may start asking the two players questions privately — what is your gem afraid of right now? — and using those answers to create tension in the fiction.

---

## The Player Choice Moment

When Harmony hits zero it doesn't immediately split. Both players simultaneously and privately decide: hold together or split. Write the choice down or cover it. Reveal at the same time.

**Both choose to hold:** Burn the last box. The fusion stays but every Harmony check from here is rolled at disadvantage, and the GM gets to ask one probing question of each player privately before every scene. The clock is ticking.

**Both choose to split:** Clean voluntary defusion. Both gems emerge intact, probably staggered. This is the better mechanical outcome — both gems recover faster from a willing split than a collapse.

**One holds, one splits:** The fusion violently destabilizes. Both gems are poofed. Their gems crack slightly — a temporary penalty that heals through downtime and a scene of reconnection. This is the consequence of misalignment. Not punishment. Story.

---

## Recovering Harmony

Harmony doesn't recover passively. It recovers through connection scenes — structured roleplay moments between the two gems while unfused, where they talk, argue, cry, laugh, or do something meaningful together. Each such scene, played earnestly (GM's call, with a light touch), restores one Harmony box.

Fusion is a resource you invest in between sessions, not just a power you use in conflict. The gems who fuse most reliably are the ones whose players do the relationship work.

# PART FOUR: CHARACTER CREATION

---

## The Steps

Character creation moves through these steps in order:

1. Choose your archetype
2. Choose your gem type
3. Assign your stats
4. Choose your weapon
5. Note your core power and choose your developed power
6. Write your signature move
7. Establish your starting Bonds
8. Answer your backstory prompts

---

## Step One: Archetype

Your archetype is the Diamond whose logic shaped you — the philosophy you were built inside, even if you're running from it now. It determines your starting stat ceilings and gives you an affiliation, a starting move, and a shadow: the specific thing you need to do before a suppressed stat can grow past its ceiling.

Archetypes don't determine your gem type. They describe the conditioning you're working with or against.

---

### LEADER — White Diamond's Logic
*"Every gem has a purpose. Including you."*

White Diamond's influence is the belief that order is love — that hierarchy exists to protect gems from the chaos of being undefined. A Leader has internalized this completely, even in rebellion. They organize others not because they're controlling but because a gem without structure feels to them like a gem in danger.

**Stat ceilings:** Form 3, Clarity 4, Resonance 2, Radiance 3, Resolve 4

**Affiliation — The Architecture:** You have organizational knowledge. Chain of command structures, patrol schedules, resource networks. You know how Homeworld thinks institutionally. This makes you invaluable for planning and terrifying to enemies who underestimate how well you understand their systems.

**Starting move — Hold the Line:** Once per session you may declare a course of action for the group. Every gem who follows it adds one bonus die to their first roll in that action, regardless of Bond. Gems who don't follow it add nothing — and you know who didn't.

**The shadow:** A Leader's Resonance limit isn't permanent, but growing past 2 requires a specific moment in the fiction: someone else being right, without you framing it as your decision. That scene must happen before the stat can advance.

---

### SOLDIER — Yellow Diamond's Logic
*"Hesitation is the only failure."*

Yellow Diamond's philosophy is efficiency as mercy — sentimentality wastes time, and wasted time costs gems their lives. A Soldier has a direct relationship with purpose. They act. They assess after. Emotion isn't absent — it's deferred, always deferred, until there's no longer a crisis to justify deferring it.

**Stat ceilings:** Form 5, Clarity 3, Resonance 2, Radiance 2, Resolve 4

**Affiliation — The Front:** You know other soldiers — current ones, former ones, ones quietly asking questions they're not supposed to ask. You can make contact with Homeworld military gems without immediately triggering alarm. That access is fragile and GM-tracked.

**Starting move — First Strike:** When you act before anyone else in a scene — before a plan is formed, before the situation is fully assessed — you roll Form with an extra die. Clean success means you set the terms of the conflict. A miss means you've committed everyone before they were ready.

**The shadow:** A Soldier's Radiance can't advance past 2 until they've done something with no tactical justification — purely expressive, purely themselves, serving no mission objective. It needs to cost them something, even something small.

---

### SCHOLAR — Blue Diamond's Logic
*"To understand is to preserve."*

Blue Diamond grieves. She collects — cultures, artifacts, histories — because she cannot stop things from ending, but she can remember them. A Scholar carries this orientation: everything is worth understanding, and understanding is its own form of care. They are the gems most likely to empathize with an enemy mid-fight.

**Stat ceilings:** Form 2, Clarity 5, Resonance 3, Radiance 3, Resolve 3

**Affiliation — The Archive:** You have access to knowledge others don't. Gem history, corrupted gem taxonomy, pre-war records, planetary surveys. Once per session you may ask the GM one question about the world's history or a gem's origins, and they must answer honestly — though not necessarily completely.

**Starting move — Read the Room:** When you enter a new scene, you may ask the GM one free question about what's really happening beneath the surface — an emotional current, a hidden agenda, a structural weakness. This costs nothing. It happens before anyone rolls.

**The shadow:** A Scholar's Resolve cap of 3 reflects Blue Diamond's core wound — the belief that loss is inevitable and resistance is a form of grief. Growing past 3 requires a moment where the Scholar chooses to fight for something rather than preserve it. Active rather than archival. The choice has to hurt a little.

---

### DIPLOMAT — Pink Diamond's Logic
*"But what do they want?"*

Pink Diamond, before her logic curdled into control, was genuinely curious about other gems' inner lives. A Diplomat carries that original impulse: every conflict has a conversation underneath it, and finding that conversation is the real work. They are not naive. They know connection can be weaponized. They do it anyway.

**Stat ceilings:** Form 2, Clarity 3, Resonance 5, Radiance 3, Resolve 3

**Affiliation — The Network:** You have contacts across factions — gems who owe you, trust you, or don't know yet that you've switched sides. Once per session you may invoke a contact for information, access, or a favor. The GM determines what that contact can realistically provide and what it might cost.

**Starting move — Open Channel:** When you attempt to de-escalate a conflict through conversation rather than force, you roll Resonance with an extra die. On a Resonance result, the enemy doesn't just stand down — they tell you something true about themselves or their orders.

**The shadow:** Playing a Diplomat in a world where Pink Diamond is the villain is complicated. Your archetype shares her original logic. Gems who know this may not trust you immediately. The GM may occasionally have NPCs clock the resemblance between your instincts and hers. That's not a penalty. It's a story.

---

### REBEL — No Diamond's Logic
*"I'm figuring it out."*

No sanctioned purpose. No patron Diamond's philosophy shaping your defaults. You emerged, escaped, or were never properly categorized, and the Authority's fingerprints on you are incomplete. That makes you unpredictable, adaptable, and genuinely free — at the cost of having no institutional support, no inherited knowledge, and no network to fall back on.

**Stat ceilings:** Form 4, Clarity 4, Resonance 4, Radiance 4, Resolve 4

No stat is capped at 5 at creation — that ceiling requires the full arc of play to reach, because it has to be entirely self-built. But no stat is hard-capped low either.

**Affiliation — None.** No network, no archive, no contacts. Instead you have an open question on your sheet — something you don't know about yourself yet. At character creation you write one thing your gem hasn't figured out: about their past, their purpose, or what they're capable of. The GM holds the answer. It surfaces in play.

**Starting move — Improvise:** Once per session, when you do something no trained gem of any Diamond's court would think to try, you may roll Radiance instead of whatever stat would normally apply. If it works, it works in a way that surprises even you.

---

## Step Two: Gem Type

Choose your gem type from the list below. Each gem type lists its recommended archetypes — the combinations that make immediate sense given Homeworld's logic. Playing outside the recommended archetypes is possible but requires GM approval and a backstory explanation. That explanation is usually the most interesting thing about the character.

Rebel is available as an option for any gem type, and is listed explicitly only for gems where it needs no explanation at all.

If you want to play a gem not on this list — including defective or off-color gems — talk to your GM. The list covers the most common types. Rarer gem types are welcome at most tables with a bit more backstory conversation first.

---

### LEADER GEMS — White Diamond's Court

**Diamond**
*(Leader only — Rebel requires explicit GM and full table approval)*
The apex of Homeworld's hierarchy. Playing a Diamond is the most significant character choice in the game and requires full table buy-in. A Diamond Rebel represents the most seismic possible break from assigned role.

**Goshenite**
*(Leader only — Rebel requires GM approval)*
Assessors who determine other gems' placement and worth within the hierarchy. Entirely defined by their function — one of the most rigid starting points, and potentially the most compelling Rebel arc. A Goshenite who turned has spent their entire existence measuring others and finally turned that judgment on the system itself.

**Howlite**
*(Leader or Scholar)*
Record keepers and institutional archivists. They know better than anyone what the official histories leave out. A Howlite Scholar is a gem sitting on a mountain of suppressed truth and deciding what to do with it.

**Spinel**
*(Leader or Diplomat)*
Companion gems built for devotion. Their organizational instincts come from structuring everything around a single relationship until care and control became indistinguishable. A Spinel Rebel is a gem whose devotion was abandoned and who had to build a self from nothing.

**Moonstone**
*(Leader or Diplomat)*
Advisors and strategic intermediaries in White Diamond's court. They think in systems but can't stop noticing what those systems cost the gems inside them. A Moonstone who became a Diplomat did so because observation eventually became conscience.

**Selenite**
*(Leader or Scholar)*
White court advisors of exceptional clarity who interpret patterns rather than keep records. Where Howlite archives, Selenite reads what the archive means. Meticulous, exacting, quietly aware of everything in a room.

---

### SOLDIER GEMS — Yellow Diamond's Court

**Topaz**
*(Soldier only — Rebel requires GM approval)*
Enforcers and containment specialists. Topazes typically come in pairs and their fusion is factory-standard — a Topaz who fuses freely and intentionally is already a small act of rebellion. Difficult to play outside Soldier without significant backstory work.

**Jasper**
*(Soldier or Rebel)*
Front-line warriors built for glory and given endless war. A Jasper's need for purpose is the most intense on the list. A Jasper Rebel is a gem trying to figure out who they are when the war they were built for has no use for them anymore.

**Ruby**
*(Soldier or Rebel)*
Guards deployed in units, often fused for tactical purposes. Considered interchangeable by Homeworld. A Ruby Rebel is a gem who realized that being told you're replaceable is a reason to leave, not a fact about your worth.

**Amethyst**
*(Soldier or Rebel)*
Quartz soldiers from specific kindergartens. Those from unsanctioned sites are anomalous by birth. An Amethyst Rebel often doesn't have to do much to earn the title — Homeworld already considered them a mistake, which is a strange kind of freedom.

**Bismuth**
*(Soldier or Leader)*
Builder-gems who construct Homeworld's weapons and infrastructure. A craftsperson's relationship with purpose. A Bismuth who turns usually does so with a specific ideology rather than a personal wound.

**Agate**
*(Soldier or Leader)*
Overseers who manage other gems' conduct and compliance — administrators of discipline, not warriors. An Agate Rebel has usually seen too clearly what they were actually doing on Homeworld's behalf.

**Onyx**
*(Soldier or Rebel)*
Heavy operatives deployed alone for work that isn't discussed afterward. An Onyx Rebel carries knowledge of what Homeworld actually does when it thinks no one is watching.

**Hessonite**
*(Soldier or Leader)*
Field commanders. They lead because command is a tactical function. A Hessonite Leader is a gem who started thinking strategically and couldn't stop applying it to everything — including Homeworld's structure.

---

### SCHOLAR GEMS — Blue Diamond's Court

**Sapphire**
*(Scholar or Diplomat)*
Future-seers of aristocratic bearing. The Scholar tendency is about the burden of knowing; the Diplomat alternative is about what you choose to say when you know more than anyone else in the room.

**Lapis Lazuli**
*(Scholar or Rebel)*
Terraformers deployed alone across vast distances. A Lapis Scholar has had enormous time with the universe and found it endlessly interesting. A Lapis Rebel has had the same time with their own thoughts and found Homeworld's logic increasingly difficult to justify.

**Aquamarine**
*(Scholar or Soldier)*
Hunters and retrievers — Scholars who do field work. Small, precise, considerably colder than their court assignment suggests.

**Zircon**
*(Scholar or Diplomat)*
Legal minds who know the rules better than anyone and are therefore best positioned to find the gaps. A Zircon who defects often does so because they argued a case they knew was right and lost anyway.

**Labradorite**
*(Scholar or Rebel)*
Rare deep-field researchers sent to study phenomena at the outer edges of Homeworld's reach. They've seen things that made the empire look very small.

**Iolite**
*(Scholar or Leader)*
Strategic analysts who think in structures rather than feelings. The grief that permeates Blue Diamond's court sits oddly on them — they understand it but don't quite share it, which makes them quietly isolated.

**Nephrite**
*(Scholar or Soldier)*
Navigation and mapping specialists — Scholars posted at the front. A Nephrite Scholar is a gem whose interest in charting the universe extended past sanctioned boundaries.

---

### DIPLOMAT GEMS — Pink Diamond's Court

**Rose Quartz**
*(Diplomat or Soldier)*
A complicated gem type in this AU. Made for Pink Diamond and carrying that weight. Their Diplomat instinct runs toward care and protection; their Soldier alternative reflects that their form is a warrior's even when their impulse isn't.

**Emerald**
*(Diplomat or Leader)*
High-status court gems of significant prestige. An Emerald who rebels is giving up more than almost anyone else on this list, which means they usually have a very specific and deeply personal reason.

**Morganite**
*(Diplomat or Leader)*
Court supervisors who manage other gems' assignments and wellbeing — or the appearance of it. A Morganite Rebel is a gem who was trusted enough to see behind the curtain and didn't like what they found.

**Kunzite**
*(Diplomat or Scholar)*
Court facilitators who move between factions carrying goodwill and information. Good at being whatever a room needs — genuine adaptability or a form of self-erasure, depending on the gem and the day.

**Tourmaline**
*(Diplomat or Rebel)*
Gems who exist across multiple registers — tourmaline comes in more colors than any other stone, and Tourmaline gems reflect this. They don't fit cleanly into any single court's logic, which makes them natural diplomats and natural suspects.

**Turquoise**
*(Diplomat or Scholar)*
Some of the oldest gems in circulation, predating the current Diamond authority's structure. They carry cultural knowledge that predates current orthodoxy and move between factions with weathered patience.

---

### CROSS-COURT GEMS

**Pearl**
*(Any archetype except Soldier)*
Made-to-order servants assigned across all courts. The most flexible gem type — their archetype is almost entirely determined by who they've been with and what that relationship did to them. Nearly every Pearl has something to prove or something to grieve. A Pearl Rebel has usually had to dismantle the belief that having a self is presumptuous.

**Peridot**
*(Scholar or Soldier)*
Technicians and kindergarten engineers. More Scholar in inclination than Soldier in function, but classified under Yellow Diamond's utility logic. The tension between actual inclination and assigned role is baked in from the start.

**Jade**
*(Diplomat or Scholar)*
Gems who occupy liminal social positions — valued but never quite central, present across courts but always slightly external to power. Wisdom earned from watching rather than participating.

**Citrine**
*(Soldier or Diplomat)*
Versatile gems who serve across Yellow and Pink Diamond's courts. Comfortable in multiple roles — which Homeworld treats as utility and Citrine often experiences as a lack of clear identity.

## Step Three: Stat Assignment

You have five points to distribute across your five stats — Form, Clarity, Resonance, Radiance, and Resolve. Place them however you like within your archetype's starting ceilings. No stat may exceed 4 at character creation regardless of archetype. The road to 5 runs through play.

A stat at 0 is valid. It means something — your gem has been conditioned out of that capability, or it simply never developed. A 0 is not a gap waiting to be filled. It's a story waiting to be told.

---

### The Optional Zero

You may voluntarily set one stat to 0 and lock it there at creation. In exchange, you gain a sixth point to spend on your remaining stats.

This represents active suppression — something Homeworld specifically conditioned out of you, or a wound deep enough that the capability genuinely isn't there yet. You must write a sentence on your sheet explaining why. Not a paragraph. A sentence. Something true.

*"She was told that caring about others was a Pearl's weakness, and she believed it."*
*"Three thousand years as a sole operator left him with no practice being known."*
*"They were never allowed to want anything for themselves."*

The sentence matters because it tells the table where one of your most important character arcs lives. A stat at mandatory 0 advances through play like any other stat, but the first point into it should feel like a scene, not a checkbox.

---

### Example Builds

**A Ruby who stayed a soldier too long**
Takes the optional 0 in Radiance: Form 4, Clarity 1, Resonance 1, Radiance 0, Resolve 1. Exceptional at exactly one thing. The Radiance 0 sentence: *"She was never asked what she wanted to be."*

**A Pearl building herself from scratch**
Takes the optional 0 in Form: Clarity 2, Resonance 3, Radiance 2, Form 0, Resolve 0. Fragile in every physical sense, more connected than almost any gem at the table. The Form 0 sentence: *"Pearls aren't supposed to fight. She's still deciding if that was a rule or a fact."*

**A Sapphire Scholar who sees too much**
Takes the optional 0 in Form: Clarity 4, Resonance 1, Resolve 2, Form 0, Radiance 0. The Form 0 sentence: *"She has never once needed to hit anything. She's not sure that makes her lucky."*

## Step Four: Weapon

Every gem summons a weapon from their gem. The weapon on your sheet is not mandatory — it's what you default to, what your gem reaches for first. In the fiction you could summon something different under extreme circumstances, but your listed weapon is the one that feels like yours.

Choose from your gem type's recommended weapons or, with GM approval, something different. An Amethyst who sneaks around with a dagger is more interesting than a list can anticipate. The list is a starting point.

---

### Weapon Tags

Weapons have one or two tags. Tags don't add dice — they determine what's possible in a scene. A concealed weapon can be drawn where a visible one couldn't. The GM uses tags to open or close narrative options, not to adjudicate rolls.

**Reach** — extends well beyond arm's length. Engage targets at distance, create space, strike from unexpected angles. Unwieldy in close quarters.

**Concealed** — small or subtle enough to go unnoticed when not summoned. Can be drawn in situations where a visible weapon would cause alarm.

**Binding** — designed to restrain rather than destroy. Captures or immobilizes without shattering. Less effective against a gem who'd rather be shattered than caught.

**Area** — affects a space rather than a single target. Harder to aim precisely, harder to ignore entirely.

**Piercing** — bypasses physical barriers that would stop other attacks. Doesn't pierce gem powers directly.

**Paired** — two weapons simultaneously. Split attention across multiple targets or cover angles a single weapon couldn't.

**Heavy** — enough force to move large objects, break structures, or destabilize a fusion through sheer impact. Slow to reposition.

**Finesse** — rewards precision over power. In situations calling for surgical accuracy, the GM may grant a bonus die.

## Step Five: Gem Powers

Every gem type has a core power — one thing this gem type always has, not a choice. It's just true. A Sapphire always has some degree of future vision. A Lapis always controls water.

Beyond the core, each gem type has two or three developed powers to choose from. These represent how your specific gem has grown or diverged from the baseline.

---

### Powers by Gem Type

---

**DIAMOND**

*Core — Diamond resonance:* Your voice carries authority at a frequency other gems feel physically. You can issue a command that bypasses conscious resistance — not mind control, but a compulsion that costs a gem significant Resolve to ignore. Against gems conditioned to Diamond authority this is harder to resist. Against gems who have fully broken from Homeworld it may not work at all.

*Developed — choose one:*
- Corruption wave (controlled): A focused burst that overwhelms a gem's form temporarily without corrupting — destabilizing rather than harming.
- Light manifestation: You create fully realized constructs of hard light — not just weapons but structures, illusions, environments. Limited by your Radiance.

---

**GOSHENITE**

*Core — Assessment:* You evaluate any gem's approximate stat spread at a glance — not exact numbers but a true sense of strengths and vulnerabilities. Takes a moment of focused observation and a Clarity roll. You cannot assess fusions this way. They read as something genuinely new.

*Developed — choose one:*
- Devalue: You identify and name a gem's conditioned weakness with such precision that they roll their next action against that weakness with one fewer die.
- Reassign: You issue bureaucratically-framed commands that gems conditioned to Homeworld's hierarchy find difficult to parse as hostile.

---

**HOWLITE**

*Core — Institutional memory:* Perfect recall of everything you've ever recorded and much you've witnessed. Once per scene you may ask the GM one question about Homeworld's history, structures, or procedures. The answer is always something your gem plausibly could have archived.

*Developed — choose one:*
- Rewrite: You alter documents and data constructs with a touch. Forgeries you create are essentially undetectable to anyone who doesn't already know the truth.
- Erase: You suppress a specific memory from a willing or incapacitated gem — not permanently, but long enough to matter. Unwilling targets require a contested Clarity roll.

---

**SPINEL**

*Core — Elasticity:* Your form is more malleable than other gems. You can stretch, compress, and reshape your body beyond normal shapeshifting — changing physical geometry, not appearance. Fit through impossible spaces, absorb impacts, extend your reach significantly.

*Developed — choose one:*
- Reset touch: A controlled application of rejuvenator energy through contact. You temporarily suppress a gem's most recently developed capability. It returns, but not immediately.
- Transformation: Your shapeshifting extends to full form changes — appearance, voice, apparent gem type. Maintaining a transformation requires concentration and a Radiance roll for anything longer than a scene.

---

**MOONSTONE**

*Core — Phase shift:* You become partially incorporeal — not invisible, but difficult to physically interact with. Attacks pass through you at reduced effect. You cannot attack while phased. You can move through thin barriers.

*Developed — choose one:*
- Reflection: You redirect a gem power attack back toward its source. Requires a Clarity roll and precise timing.
- Lunar pull: A subtle gravitational influence making gems near you slightly more inclined toward honesty and calm. Not compulsion — a mood. Detectable by gems with strong Clarity.

---

**SELENITE**

*Core — Structural clarity:* You perceive the underlying architecture of situations, systems, and gems — load-bearing elements, stress fractures, points of maximum vulnerability. Once per scene you may identify the single most vulnerable point in whatever you're analyzing.

*Developed — choose one:*
- Light amplification: You gather and focus ambient light into a damaging beam. Diminished in darkness; significantly more dangerous in bright conditions.
- Resonant frequency: You identify and produce the specific frequency that destabilizes a particular gem construct or structure — not living gems, built things.

---

**TOPAZ**

*Core — Containment field:* You generate a hardened energy construct around a target that immobilizes them completely. Requires physical contact to initiate and a Form roll to maintain. The field doesn't harm — it holds.

*Developed — choose one:*
- Fusion pressure: When fused with another Topaz (or any gem with GM approval), you can extend the containment field to cover a much larger area.
- Amber seal: A more permanent version of containment, nearly impossible to break from within. Used rarely. Has obvious ethical implications.

---

**JASPER**

*Core — Perfect quartz:* Your form is exceptionally resilient. Once per conflict you may ignore the mechanical effects of a single significant hit — taking it narratively but suffering no Harmony loss or stat penalties from it.

*Developed — choose one:*
- Charge: You lower your head and close distance at full force, crashing through anything between you and your target. On a clean success the target is knocked back and destabilized — their next roll is at a disadvantage. You cannot easily stop or redirect mid-charge.
- Relentless: Once per conflict, when you are poofed, you may return to the scene at Form 1 rather than sitting out. You don't resist the poof — you simply refuse to stay down.

---

**RUBY**

*Core — Heat:* You generate intense heat from your gem — superheated strikes, close-range fire projection, melting through certain barriers. Sustained heat output requires concentration and reduces your ability to maintain form under other pressure simultaneously.

*Developed — choose one:*
- Thermal vision: You perceive heat signatures — useful for tracking, detecting hidden gems, or reading emotional states. Works in darkness. Doesn't work on Homeworld constructs that don't generate heat.
- Rage ignition: When your Resolve drops below 2, your heat output doubles automatically. You don't control this. More dangerous, significantly harder to reason with.

---

**AMETHYST**

*Core — Shapeshifting:* Your shapeshifting is more fluid and faster than other gems. You can change form mid-combat, hold a shape longer under pressure, and copy appearances with precision. Gem powers don't transfer with the appearance.

*Developed — choose one:*
- Size manipulation: You grow significantly larger or compress significantly smaller than your base form. Large form adds Heavy to any attack. Small form adds Concealed to your presence entirely.
- Spin dash: You compress your form and launch as a spinning projectile. Devastating on impact, difficult to steer. You are the area.

---

**BISMUTH**

*Core — Constructs:* You shape gem-metal and hard light into structures, weapons, and tools on the fly. In combat this means environmental manipulation — barriers, traps, improvised weapons for allies. Outside combat your construction ability is nearly limitless given time and materials.

*Developed — choose one:*
- Forge: You permanently upgrade another gem's summoned weapon — adding a tag or enhancing an existing one. Requires time, physical contact with the weapon, and a scene of focused work. Lasts until the weapon is re-summoned from scratch.
- Deconstruct: You break down gem constructs by identifying their resonant frequency. Faster and more precise than simply hitting them.

---

**AGATE**

*Core — Aura of authority:* Your presence carries weight other gems feel as pressure. Gems conditioned to Homeworld hierarchy find your direct instructions difficult to casually ignore. A passive scene condition: unconditioned gems roll Resolve to actively defy you.

*Developed — choose one:*
- Terrify: A focused blast of your authority aura that temporarily overwhelms a single target's ability to access their Form or Resolve cleanly. One exchange.
- Rally: The same aura turned toward allies. Once per conflict you steady a flagging gem — restoring one Harmony to a fusion or giving an ally a reroll on a failed Resolve check.

---

**ONYX**

*Core — Shadow form:* Nearly invisible in low-light conditions. In full darkness completely undetectable by sight. Bright light suppresses this entirely.

*Developed — choose one:*
- Dark construct: You create objects from solidified shadow — barriers, decoys, crude tools. Fully physical until light touches them.
- Null presence: Beyond visual concealment, you suppress your gem's signature — other gems cannot sense your presence, power, or emotional state. Useful for infiltration. Unsettling to allies.

---

**HESSONITE**

*Core — Command presence:* Your voice in conflict carries tactical authority. Once per conflict you issue a tactical directive that gives every ally who follows it a bonus die on their next roll. Allies who deeply trust you get it even on a partial follow.

*Developed — choose one:*
- Read the field: At the start of any conflict you may ask the GM two tactical questions — enemy numbers, positions, capabilities, objectives. True as of that moment.
- Rally point: You designate a position as a rally point. Allies who reach it recover one point of Form damage and gain a bonus die on their next Resolve roll. One rally point active at a time.

---

**SAPPHIRE**

*Core — Future vision:* You see what is likely to happen before it does. Once per conflict you may declare that you knew something was coming — a trap, an ambush, a lie — even if you didn't say so at the time. Outside combat you can attempt deliberate visions with a Clarity roll; further ahead means less certainty.

*Developed — choose one:*
- Shared vision: You show another gem a future you've seen — through touch, eye contact, or description. What they do with it is their choice.
- Ice mastery: Your control over cold extends beyond weapon summoning — freeze liquids, lower temperatures, create barriers, encase targets. Sustained effects require concentration.

---

**LAPIS LAZULI**

*Core — Hydrokinesis:* You control water — any water, in any form, within sight. Scale scales with Form and emotional state. At full strength you can move oceans. At low Harmony your control becomes erratic. You can extract water from damp air at significant effort.

*Developed — choose one:*
- Wings: Hard water wings allow flight. Airborne, your hydrokinesis range extends significantly. Also more exposed.
- Mirror prison: You trap a gem in a reflective water construct — not just restrained but isolated, cut off from outside contact. More complete than standard binding. More ethically significant. You know this from experience.

---

**AQUAMARINE**

*Core — Flight:* You fly naturally and with precision — not wings, self-propelled. Your small size and flight make you genuinely difficult to catch. You can hover indefinitely, move at significant speed, operate in environments that ground-based gems can't.

*Developed — choose one:*
- Retrieval construct: An enhanced version of your wand's function — fires a capturing construct that pursues a target for a short distance before dissipating.
- Sonic attack: At close range a high-frequency burst that disrupts a gem's ability to maintain their form. Doesn't poof — destabilizes. Makes their next roll significantly harder.

---

**ZIRCON**

*Core — Legal mind:* You construct an airtight argument under any circumstances. Once per scene you reframe a situation — legally, logically, rhetorically — in a way that changes what options are available. Doesn't create facts. Changes how existing facts are understood.

*Developed — choose one:*
- Expose contradiction: You identify and name the logical flaw in an opponent's position with enough precision that they lose access to that argument for the rest of the scene.
- Reasonable doubt: You introduce enough uncertainty about an established fact that it can no longer be used against you or your allies in the current situation.

---

**LABRADORITE**

*Core — Labradorescence:* You refract and manipulate light — bending it for concealment, focusing it into blinding flashes, creating visual phenomena indistinguishable from reality to any gem not specifically trained to detect illusions. Sustained illusions require concentration.

*Developed — choose one:*
- Deep field vision: You perceive things at extreme distances and detect phenomena other gems can't register — warp signatures, corruption traces, gem power residue.
- Spectral form: You make yourself appear to be somewhere you aren't — a full visual duplicate, in place, doing something plausible, while you're elsewhere. No mass. Lasts until something tries to touch it.

---

**IOLITE**

*Core — Trichroism:* Your perception covers three visual spectra simultaneously — visible, near-infrared, and ultraviolet. Hidden writing, concealed gems, invisible constructs, and emotional heat signatures are all visible to you.

*Developed — choose one:*
- Structural analysis: You look at any system and identify both its function and its failure points. Slower than Selenite's clarity but precise enough for detailed planning.
- Directional sense: You always know your exact position relative to any point you've previously been. You cannot be lost. You build a complete spatial map as you move through unfamiliar locations.

---

**NEPHRITE**

*Core — Navigation:* You always know exactly where you are in space — planetary coordinates, stellar position, warp coordinates. You calculate routes through warp with precision other gems can't match and detect warp instability before it becomes dangerous.

*Developed — choose one:*
- Warp detection: You sense active warp pads and signatures within a significant radius. You know when someone has recently warped and can identify where they went if you reach the pad quickly enough.
- Aerial form: You take a partial flight form closer to your ship's silhouette. Faster than walking, useful for reconnaissance, uncomfortable to maintain long.

---

**PERIDOT**

*Core — Ferrokinesis:* You control metal and metal-adjacent materials at range — with technical precision. You manipulate Homeworld technology, disassemble constructs, retrieve objects, and fight without closing distance. Era 2 Peridots developed this as a compensation. It has become something genuinely their own.

*Developed — choose one:*
- Technology interface: You connect directly with Homeworld technology through touch — reading, operating, and reprogramming systems. Non-Homeworld technology requires more effort and a Clarity roll.
- Tactical analysis: Your engineering mind extends to conflict. Once per conflict the GM tells you one true thing about the most effective path through the current situation.

---

**ROSE QUARTZ**

*Core — Healing:* You heal physical damage to gem forms — closing cracks, restoring poofed gems more quickly, partially repairing cracked gems. You cannot heal a shattered gem. Healing requires physical contact and costs you Form temporarily — you give some of your own stability to restore theirs.

*Developed — choose one:*
- Healing tears: Your healing extends to emotional damage — not erasing it, but making it survivable. A gem in crisis who receives your tears can function through something that would otherwise incapacitate them. The pain is still there. They can move through it.
- Protective bubble: You generate a hard-light shield around another gem or group. While maintaining it you can't attack.

---

**EMERALD**

*Core — Command authority:* You have access to Homeworld shipping and transport networks — authorization codes, routing, docking clearances. Once per session you invoke a legitimate-sounding Homeworld authorization that gets you or your party through a bureaucratic obstacle.

*Developed — choose one:*
- Status projection: You make any gem you're with appear to be higher status than they are. Works until someone checks the records or a genuinely high-status gem looks closely.
- Void the authorization: You revoke someone else's clearances, freeze their access, or flag their authorization as compromised.

---

**MORGANITE**

*Core — Oversight:* You know the official status, assignment, and recorded history of any gem you've interacted with through Homeworld's systems. You also know when a gem's actual behavior is deviating from their recorded function.

*Developed — choose one:*
- Reassignment order: You issue a reassignment that Homeworld-conditioned gems feel compelled to follow — a bureaucratic compulsion that feels like paperwork rather than an order.
- Cover story: You construct a complete false record for a gem or group — fake assignment, fake history, fake authorization. Holds up to casual scrutiny. Won't survive thorough investigation.

---

**KUNZITE**

*Core — Emotional fluency:* You read emotional states with precision through observation — behavior, microexpression, word choice, posture. You always know the general emotional state of any gem you're in direct conversation with, and can identify when they're suppressing something significant. Observation, not intrusion.

*Developed — choose one:*
- Mirror affect: You reflect a gem's emotional state back at them with amplified clarity — making them feel what they're actually feeling more intensely, rather than what they're performing. Can be overwhelming if used without care.
- Neutral ground: You establish a conversational space in which neither party feels threatened enough to escalate. Lasts as long as both parties find it useful and you maintain it actively.

---

**TOURMALINE**

*Core — Spectrum shift:* You alter your own coloring, apparent gem type, and surface presentation at will — more comprehensive than standard shapeshifting, specifically covering the social signals other gems use to place you in the hierarchy. Your actual gem doesn't change and can be detected by touch or a careful Clarity roll.

*Developed — choose one:*
- Broad reception: You communicate meaningfully with gems from any court without triggering the instinctive wariness that cross-court interaction usually produces. You read as belonging wherever you are, at least initially.
- Frequency match: You identify the specific social register a gem is operating on — what they need to hear, what authority they're conditioned to respect — and match it precisely.

---

**TURQUOISE**

*Core — Ancient knowing:* You have existed through multiple eras of Homeworld's history. Once per session you identify a precedent, loophole, or historical exception that technically applies to the current situation — something that predates current law and hasn't been formally rescinded.

*Developed — choose one:*
- Cultural memory: You know histories that predate current Homeworld records. The GM tells you one true suppressed historical fact per session.
- Weathered form: Your long existence has made your physical form exceptionally stable. Once per conflict you may re-enter a scene after being poofed without the usual recovery time.

---

**PEARL**

*Core — Holographic projection:* You project detailed holograms from your gem — recordings, maps, schematics, visual information. Purely visual, no physical presence. You can project decoys of yourself or others. Resolution and detail limited only by Clarity.

*Developed — choose one:*
- Subspace storage: You store objects inside your gem — physically, not as projections. Limited but significant capacity. Completely undetectable. Retrieved instantly.
- Skill absorption: You learn physical and technical skills by observing them once, retaining them permanently. This gives you knowledge, not stats. A Pearl who has watched a thousand sword fights knows what good technique looks like even if their Form is 1.

---

**JADE**

*Core — Durability:* Your physical form is exceptionally dense and stable. Once per conflict when you would be poofed, you may instead remain present at 1 Form — barely holding together but still in the fight.

*Developed — choose one:*
- Grounding presence: Allies in your immediate vicinity gain a bonus die on Resolve rolls to resist being poofed or destabilized while you're standing.
- Jade sense: Your long experience reading situations from the outside gives you acute awareness of deception. You can always tell when a gem is performing rather than being genuine — not what the truth is, just that what you're seeing isn't it.

---

**CITRINE**

*Core — Adaptability:* Once per conflict you switch which stat you're rolling for a physical action — using Clarity instead of Form for a precise strike, Resolve instead of Form to push through on willpower. You've filled enough roles to know the right tool isn't always the obvious one.

*Developed — choose one:*
- Read the assignment: Once per session the GM tells you what this scene most needs from the party.
- Warm presence: Gems who have just met you treat you as though they've known you slightly longer than they have. Especially useful in infiltration and first contact situations.

## Step Six: Signature Move

Your signature move is the one thing that is mechanically, specifically, irreducibly you. Not your gem type — you. It represents something you've figured out through experience, loss, relationship, or stubbornness that no list of powers could have given you.

It is written by you, approved by your GM, and subject to change as your gem changes.

---

### Writing Your Signature Move

Use this template:

*"When [specific circumstance], I can [effect]. [Optional: cost or condition]."*

The circumstance should be specific enough that it doesn't trigger constantly — once or twice a session at most — but broad enough that it comes up naturally in play. The effect should do one clear thing. The cost makes it a choice rather than a reflex.

The circumstance is the most important part. It should say something true about your gem — about what situations call something out of them, what conditions make them exceptional.

---

### Examples

*"When I step between someone and the thing coming for them, I take the hit without rolling. It lands — but I don't go down."*

*"When I've been dismissed or underestimated by someone in this scene, my next roll gets bonus dice equal to how many times they've done it."*

*"When I tell the truth about something I've been hiding, every gem who hears it gets a bonus die on their next Resonance roll."*

*"When everything has failed and I'm the last one standing, I don't poof. I get one more action at full capacity before my form gives out."*

*"When I fuse with another gem, the fusion begins with one additional Harmony box."*

*"When I ask someone what they want — genuinely, without agenda — and wait for the real answer, they give it to me."*

*"When I do something my gem type was never supposed to do, I roll Radiance instead of the relevant stat and add my Radiance to the pool."*

---

### GM Approval

Your GM approves your signature move before play begins. They're checking three things:

**Is the circumstance specific enough?** If it could apply to more than half the scenes in a session, it needs narrowing.

**Is the effect one clear thing?** If you need more than one sentence to describe what it does, it does too much.

**Is it true to your gem?** Not your gem type — this gem, as you've written them. If a different player could have written the same move for a different character without changing a word, it needs to be more personal.

The GM can suggest adjustments but shouldn't rewrite it. The move needs to come from you.

---

### Changing Your Signature Move

Your signature move can change. Gems change. A move that no longer fits your gem is worse than no move at all — it's a lie about who they are now.

To change your signature move you need two things:

**A story justification.** Something has to have happened that makes the old move no longer true or makes a new one newly true. Point to it in the fiction.

**GM approval.** Same process as the original.

When your move changes, write the old one on your sheet. Not erased — retired. It's still true that it was true once.

---

## Step Seven: Starting Bonds

You begin play with Bonds to your fellow player characters and, potentially, to key NPCs. The universal starting rule:

**One Bond may start at 3. All others start at 1 or 2. No Bond starts higher than 3.**

The single Bond at 3 requires a backstory justification — a shared history established before play begins. Write it on your sheet. Not just a number. A sentence.

*"We escaped Yellow Diamond's fleet together. We were the only two who made it to the warp pad."*

*"She was assigned to me as a containment measure. I've been trying to prove she was wrong about me for two hundred years. I think I'm starting to believe she wasn't."*

Bonds to NPCs follow the same rules. Starting at 3 with Amazonite is possible but requires a real explanation for why the rebellion's leader trusts you that much already.

Bonds are asymmetric. Your Bond with another gem is yours. Write only your own.

---

## Step Eight: Backstory Prompts

Answer these questions before your first session. Write as much or as little as feels true. There are no wrong answers, only incomplete ones.

---

### Universal Questions — Every Gem Answers These

**1. What were you made to do, and when did you first notice the gap between that and what you actually are?**
This doesn't have to be a dramatic moment. Sometimes the gap announces itself quietly — a thought you weren't supposed to have, a preference that didn't fit your function, a gem you weren't supposed to care about.

**2. What do you believe about the rebellion that you haven't said out loud yet?**
Maybe a doubt. Maybe a hope that feels too large to speak. Maybe something you think Amazonite is wrong about and haven't found the courage to raise.

**3. Name one gem — not a PC — who mattered to you before this. Where are they now?**
They don't have to be alive. They don't have to be safe. They just have to be real to you. Give the GM someone to work with.

**4. What does your gem's physical form tell other gems about you that you wish it didn't?**
Your form is a projection but it still communicates. Something about how you hold yourself, what you default to, what you can't quite hide.

**5. What would make you leave the rebellion?**
Not what you think should make you leave — what actually would. The thing that, if it happened, would break the thing keeping you here. Write it down. You don't have to show anyone.

**6. What do you want that has nothing to do with the war?**
Something small is fine. Something large is fine. Something you're not sure you're allowed to want is especially fine.

**7. What's the bravest thing you've ever done, and did anyone see it?**

---

### Leader — Additional Questions

What order did you give that you're still second-guessing?

What would it mean to you if the rebellion won but you weren't the one who led it there?

---

### Soldier — Additional Questions

What order did you carry out that you're still carrying?

What does your body know how to do that your mind has decided it won't?

---

### Scholar — Additional Questions

What do you know that you wish you didn't?

What have you been studying that has nothing to do with the rebellion and everything to do with what you're afraid of?

---

### Diplomat — Additional Questions

What do you understand about Pink Diamond that you've never told the others?

---

### Rebel — Additional Questions

The open question on your sheet — write it here in full. What don't you know about yourself yet?

Where did you sleep the first night after you left? Describe it.

Who taught you the first thing you learned that Homeworld didn't want you to know?

# PART FIVE: THE RECOMMENDED PREMISE

---

## The Amazonite Rebellion

This is a recommended starting premise. The GM is free to use it as written, adapt it freely, or build something entirely different using the system. The emotional architecture — a rebellion led by a fusion, fighting an empire that mistakes control for care — should stay. The specifics are yours to shape.

---

### Background

Pink Diamond never went to Earth. The planet was surveyed, flagged for colonization, and then deprioritized — Pink was given other assignments, other planets, other purposes. Earth exists in Homeworld's records as a pending project, unremarkable, waiting its turn.

Without Earth, there was no rebellion. Without a rebellion, the Diamond Authority's hold on the empire tightened slowly and completely over thousands of years. White Diamond's vision of perfect order has had time to mature into something that looks, from inside, almost peaceful. Every gem has a purpose. Every purpose has a place. The machinery is very quiet now.

Pink Diamond is no longer the youngest, uncertain, eager-to-please gem she once was. Millennia of rule and unchecked power have refined her original impulse — but what do they want? — into something controlling and absolute. She doesn't ask what gems want anymore. She decides. She has become convinced that she understands other gems better than they understand themselves, that her care is the only reliable care, and that any gem who claims to want something different simply hasn't been properly understood yet.

She is not cruel in the way Yellow Diamond is cruel. She is certain in a way that leaves no room for anyone else to be certain about anything.

The other Diamonds defer to her on matters of gem welfare. She has made herself indispensable to their conscience.

---

### The Rebellion

It has no official name. Homeworld calls it a contamination. The gems inside it call it, quietly, the Amazonite Rebellion — after the fusion who leads it, who appeared one day on the outskirts of a gem colony and started asking the question Pink Diamond stopped asking a long time ago.

*What do you want?*

---

### Sodalite

A former intelligence operative in Blue Diamond's court. Small — Pearl-height or just under — with the bearing of someone three times her size. Deep blue-indigo coloring, court-dressed, possessed of a quality of attention that makes the gem she's talking to feel like the only thing in the room.

Her psychic ability — a form of deep empathic reading that at its most precise allows her to surface another gem's suppressed thoughts and feelings — made her extraordinarily effective. It also made her dangerous. On a mission she won't discuss in detail, her control slipped. What she surfaced in her target wasn't information. It was something private and devastating, and it came out in front of people it shouldn't have, and the mission collapsed.

She was reassigned to Cat's Eye Chrysoberyl — administrative logic: pair the unstable psychic with an anti-psychic field. Sodalite was furious about it for approximately two hundred years and then, gradually, wasn't anymore.

**Sodalite as an NPC:** Form 1, Clarity 5, Resonance 4, Radiance 2, Resolve 3.

**Her psychic ability:** Once per scene she may attempt a deep read on any gem she's in direct contact with. On a success, she surfaces one true thing about that gem they haven't said aloud. On a Resonance result, something even they didn't fully know about themselves. On a failure, something surfaces in Sodalite instead.

---

### Cat's Eye Chrysoberyl

Large, soft, genuinely warm in a way that has nothing to do with training or strategy. Her gem produces a passive field that suppresses active gem powers within a radius roughly the size of a large room — weapons won't summon cleanly, abilities flicker, psychic contact gets static. She cannot turn it off. She has never been able to turn it off.

She doesn't read as a weapon. She reads as someone's very enthusiastic friend who happens to make everyone around her slightly less capable. This is, from her perspective, the whole problem.

The reassignment to Sodalite was framed to her as a containment assignment — keep the unstable psychic from causing further damage. She took this to mean she was being used as a suppression tool, which is accurate, and also that she was the problem being solved, which is not. It took her a long time to understand that Sodalite had chosen to stay. She's still not entirely sure she believes it.

In a fight she is almost unkillable by gem-power means — attacks that rely on summoned weapons or abilities simply don't work properly near her. Her star-shaped shield summons cleanly because it's her own weapon emerging from her own gem, partially inside her own field — a loophole that took her a long time to figure out and still delights her when she thinks about it.

**Cat's Eye Chrysoberyl as an NPC:** Form 4, Clarity 2, Resonance 3, Radiance 1, Resolve 4.

**Her suppression field:** A passive scene condition when she's present — any gem (ally or enemy) attempting to use a gem power or summoned weapon rolls with one fewer die. Cat's Eye herself is immune. Allies who have fought alongside her before can request she expand or contract the field's intensity as a Resolve roll — she's learned some control, just not complete control.

---

### Amazonite

The fusion of Sodalite and Cat's Eye Chrysoberyl. Not a compromise between them — something that emerged from the combination and couldn't have existed any other way.

She is taller than Sodalite and built more solidly than Chrysoberyl. Her coloring runs from deep teal at the crown to blue-green at the hem. She is warm and precise simultaneously. She acts decisively and feels the cost of it completely. She is the thing both of them are trying to be and can only fully be together.

She is, technically, a diva. She knows it. She leans into it carefully — just enough that it becomes a kind of permission for other gems to also take up space. If Amazonite can be this much, surely they're allowed to be a little bit more than Homeworld told them they could be. This is completely intentional.

Being Amazonite is tiring in a way that belongs specifically to her and to no one else. The passive truth field runs constantly. She is always, to some degree, holding what's true for every gem in her vicinity. She recovers in quiet. She has gotten very good at finding quiet. Sodalite and Chrysoberyl have gotten very good at making it available without being asked.

**Amazonite's unique power — Resonant Truth:** Sodalite reads what's hidden. Chrysoberyl blocks what's projected. Together they create something different: the ability to make what is already true come to the surface.

This operates on a spectrum.

*Passive — Presence:* Simply being in a space with Amazonite makes honesty feel low-cost. Gems say things they've been holding. Silences get broken. This isn't a roll — it's a scene condition. It operates all the time and Amazonite can't fully turn it off.

*Active — Surface:* A deliberate, targeted version. Amazonite focuses on a specific gem and what's true for them rises more urgently. Not forced out — strongly invited. The target can still resist, but it costs them something. Mechanically this is a Resonant Truth roll contested by the target's Resolve. On a success something true surfaces. On a Resonance result it's something the target didn't intend to surface at all. On a failure Amazonite feels the weight of what they're holding without being able to bring it out.

*Forced — The Beam:* She hates this. She uses it when someone is going to get hurt if she doesn't, and she accounts for it afterward, privately, with Sodalite. Full focused truth extraction — what the target most needs to not say comes out completely and immediately. No roll for the target. Once per session. Costs Amazonite two Harmony boxes. Requires a scene of genuine processing with at least one other gem before the next session. Using it is always a story event, never just a tool. She has used it four times in the entire history of the rebellion. She remembers each occasion precisely.

**Amazonite's three fusion stats:**
- Resonant Truth — the full spectrum of her truth ability
- Still Point — her capacity to create a genuine pause in any situation, not through emotion but through presence; a breath inside the noise where what's actually happening becomes clear
- Clarity of Purpose — what emerges when Sodalite's perception and Chrysoberyl's field combine fully; she sees through deception with surgical accuracy and asks questions instead of stating what she sees, which lands harder

**Stats:** Resonant Truth 4, Still Point 3, Clarity of Purpose 4.

**The subtle crack:** Sodalite has begun carrying a recurring doubt she hasn't named yet — she wonders whether the rebellion can win without becoming something like what it's fighting. Chrysoberyl knows Sodalite is carrying something and is giving her space to come to it. The space has become a gap. Neither of them has noticed yet that Amazonite holds herself slightly differently than she did a year ago. The GM should track this privately. It doesn't need to surface in the first arc.

---

### The Players' Position

The player characters are a newly formed cell of the rebellion — gems who found their way to Amazonite through different paths and have been placed together because Sodalite, specifically, thought they might be able to do something together that none of them could do alone. She is usually right about this.

---

### The First Arc — The Assessor

Homeworld has begun deploying a new kind of authority gem: a Goshenite who calls herself the Assessor, whose specific function is identifying and evaluating potential rebel sympathizers before they can act. She doesn't shatter. She reassigns. Gems who are flagged by the Assessor disappear into new postings, new purposes, new identities. Some of them come back wrong. Some of them don't come back at all.

The players' first arc involves understanding what the Assessor is actually doing to gems — and deciding what to do about it. It is not, at first, obvious that the answer is violence.

---

### What the GM Can Change

Everything except the core emotional architecture. The rebellion's symbol of hope should always be a fusion — a leader who only exists because two gems chose each other. That argument is the heart of what the rebellion is fighting for.

The specifics — gem types, names, courts of origin, the nature of the first arc's antagonist — are all yours. The recommended premise is a starting point, not a contract.

# APPENDIX: QUICK REFERENCE

## The Roll

Build pool: stat dice + Bond dice (one Bond only, if active)
Read highest die: 6 = success / 4–5 = success with cost / 1–3 = miss
Second die: GM may invoke for texture
Two or more 6s: Resonance result

## The Scale (Stats, Bonds, Harmony)

0 = absent / conditioned / severed
1 = nascent / fragile
2 = real / functional
3 = deep / strong
4 = foundational / defining
5 = resonant / mastery

## Fusion Harmony Triggers

- Players narratively disagree
- Significant physical damage
- Bond between constituent gems drops
- Enemy targets the relationship directly

## Harmony Track

5 boxes full = stable
2 boxes = signature moves unavailable
1 box = GM asks private questions
0 boxes = player choice moment (both hold / both split / one each)

## Advancement

One stat + one Bond per session maximum
Mark moments in play, advance at session end with table agreement
Stats and Bonds can decrease as well as increase

## Weapon Tags

Reach / Concealed / Binding / Area / Piercing / Paired / Heavy / Finesse
