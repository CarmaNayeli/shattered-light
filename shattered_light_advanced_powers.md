# SHATTERED LIGHT — ADDITIONAL DEVELOPED POWERS

These are unlockable via the XP-spend `additionalPowers` system. Each entry follows the same design constraints as the rulebook: one clear effect, specific fictional trigger, no raw dice inflation. Powers are sorted by gem type in rulebook order.

All powers marked **[Advanced]** represent deeper mastery and should cost more XP than standard unlocks.

---

## LEADER GEMS — White Diamond's Court

### DIAMOND
- **Resonant command**: Your Diamond resonance extends to written orders and recordings. A directive issued in writing carries the same compulsive weight as a spoken one. Gems who read it must spend Resolve to consciously set it aside.
- **Light prison**: You construct a hard-light containment shell around a target — complete, near-invisible, unbreakable from within without significant effort. Requires sustained concentration and a Form roll each exchange to maintain. It doesn't harm. It holds completely.
- **[Advanced] Shatter potential**: You evaluate a gem and know, with precise clarity, exactly what it would take to shatter them. The GM tells you privately. You carry this. You have never used it. The question of whether you could is a story that hasn't ended.

### GOSHENITE
- **Counter-assessment**: When another gem attempts to evaluate, read, or manipulate you, you immediately recognize the attempt and can redirect it. Roll Clarity; on a success you surface something true about them instead of yielding anything about yourself.
- **Override classification**: You formally reclassify a gem's type in Homeworld's records — removing their function-based restrictions for a period, or adding new ones. The paperwork is real. The consequences are real. It lasts until someone checks thoroughly.
- **[Advanced] Reassess the system**: Once per session you may turn your Assessment ability on an institution, faction, or mission rather than a gem. The GM gives you its approximate strengths, its suppressed weaknesses, and one thing it's afraid of.

### HOWLITE
- **Living record**: You transcribe events in real-time with perfect fidelity. Once per session, produce a document so accurate it functions as legal or testimonial proof of what occurred — in any jurisdiction that respects Homeworld's record-keeping, which is most of them.
- **Archive access**: You pull complete historical records on any location, event, or gem you've previously encountered. Once per scene, detailed enough to provide a tactical or social advantage before the situation develops.
- **[Advanced] Memory theft**: With physical contact and a contested Clarity roll, you extract a specific memory from a gem's experience and record it externally. The gem still has the memory. So do you. The copy is exact.

### SPINEL
- **Elastic anchor**: You extend a limb to bind two things together — yourself and a target, two allies, a door and its frame — and hold them in relation to each other as long as you maintain it. Requires a Form roll to break from outside. You feel it immediately if something tries.
- **Rejection pulse**: A sudden violent uncoiling of compressed form that throws everything in close range away from you simultaneously. No aiming. No targeting. You are the center and everything else is the edge.
- **[Advanced] Recorded self**: You access a version of your form from before — before whatever happened. It lasts one scene. Your stats run as they were then, for better or worse. The GM determines what "before" means for your gem specifically.

### MOONSTONE
- **Phase transfer**: While phased, you carry one willing gem partially through a solid barrier with you. They emerge on the other side disoriented — their first roll in the new location is made with one fewer die. You emerge normally.
- **Eclipse**: You phase an area of light itself, plunging a specific zone into complete and immediate darkness. You cannot phase yourself while maintaining it. Lasts until you release it or something disrupts your concentration.
- **[Advanced] Reflective field**: Your phase ability extends to creating a partial mirror around yourself for one exchange — attacks pass through and emerge from a different angle entirely. You choose where. Requires a Clarity roll to place correctly.

### SELENITE
- **Fault map**: After a scene of observation, you produce a complete internal diagram of a structure, system, or gem's capabilities — every stress point, every load-bearing element, every place where pressure would produce collapse. The GM describes what exploitation of each point would achieve.
- **Prismatic barrier**: You refract incoming light-based and energy attacks into harmless spectrum, effectively shielding against any power that operates through radiant means. Physical attacks are unaffected. The barrier is visible. Everyone in the scene knows what you're doing.
- **[Advanced] Resonant cascade**: You identify a frequency that destabilizes an entire class of construct simultaneously — not one thing, everything of that type in range. Once per conflict. The effect is brief but total.

## SOLDIER GEMS — Yellow Diamond's Court

### TOPAZ
- **Amber shell**: You coat yourself or an adjacent ally in a partial amber construct — not full containment, but substantial armor. The next significant hit against that gem is reduced narratively: it lands, but without the full mechanical consequence it would otherwise carry.
- **Chain containment**: You link two separate containment fields, making both harder to escape. What weakens one weakens the other. Breaking free of either requires overcoming both simultaneously.
- **[Advanced] Systematic containment**: Your field can be expanded to cover an entire space rather than a single target — holding everyone inside without distinguishing ally from enemy. You are also held. This is a last resort. It functions as one.

### JASPER
- **War cry**: A focused burst of aggressive presence that forces every gem in the scene to recalculate their threat assessment. Enemies must make Resolve rolls or redirect their attention toward you, regardless of what else was happening.
- **Quartz endurance**: You can take damage that would poof another gem and stay present, form cracking, at the cost of immediate Form damage instead. You don't resist the hit. You absorb it. The difference matters.
- **[Advanced] Corrupted glimpse**: You've been close enough to corruption to understand it from the inside. Once per session you describe what it felt like — the loss of self, the static — and force a Harmony check on any fusion currently in the scene.

### RUBY
- **Controlled burn**: Sustained focused heat output through direct contact. You melt through barriers, weld things shut, or assist in closing gem cracks — functioning as support for a healer. Requires concentration. You cannot fight while maintaining it.
- **Heat shield**: You project your heat outward as a defensive shell. Incoming physical attacks are significantly reduced; the attacker is burned on contact. You cannot attack while maintaining the shield.
- **[Advanced] Shared heat**: You extend your heat to an ally — not as damage, but as a stabilizing temperature that counteracts conditions (ice, paralysis, shock) that would otherwise impair them. Costs you the same Form you would spend using heat offensively.

### AMETHYST
- **Form copy**: You hold a copied appearance with sufficient precision to pass biometric gem authentication — not just visual, but the subtle light signature other gems read instinctively. It drops the moment you use any power.
- **Clustered form**: You partially fragment your presence into two simultaneous locations — not independent selves, but enough physical presence in two places at once to act from both. Costs significant Form to maintain. You feel what happens to both.
- **[Advanced] Combat forms**: Your shapeshifting extends to mid-combat form changes for tactical advantage — not full transformation but targeted alteration. Extra reach, additional limbs, density shifts. Each change costs one Form die worth of concentration. The GM determines what's achievable.

### BISMUTH
- **Rapid construction**: You build a functional structure or tool in one scene rather than over extended time. It's temporary — it won't hold beyond the session — but it holds now, and right now is what matters.
- **Alloy**: You temporarily combine two gems' summoned weapons into a single construct they wield together. Both players must agree. The combined weapon carries both weapons' tags. It lasts one scene, then separates.
- **[Advanced] Disassemble**: You take apart any gem construct — weapon, prison, architectural element — by identifying its structural logic and reversing it. Faster than simply hitting it. Works on anything built, not on living gems.

### AGATE
- **Suppress**: Your authority aura focused precisely on a single gem causes them to lose clean access to one stat for the rest of the scene. Their rolls using it are made at disadvantage. They know exactly what you did and why. That's part of it.
- **Court memory**: You invoke the precise institutional logic of Homeworld compliance protocols in a way that creates hesitation even in unconditioned gems — they have to consciously choose to override something that was built into most of them very early.
- **[Advanced] Sustained aura**: Your passive authority field intensifies. For one scene, the Resolve roll to defy your direct instructions becomes mandatory for all gems present, not just Homeworld-conditioned ones.

### ONYX
- **Vanishing act**: You conceal one other gem within your shadow presence — they gain your Concealed quality for a scene. They must remain within arm's reach. If they act, it breaks.
- **Dark resonance**: In complete darkness you perceive the emotional state of every gem within range with the precision of a Clarity 5 read — not mind-reading, but emotionally exact. Any light source breaks it entirely.
- **[Advanced] Shadow step**: You move from one area of deep shadow to another within a scene boundary without crossing the intervening space. No roll for the movement itself. Detection at the destination still applies normally.

### HESSONITE
- **Strategic withdrawal**: You organize a retreat that costs the enemy. Even as your group leaves, you ensure they lose something in the process — a position, a resource, a piece of intelligence. A miss is still a controlled loss.
- **Chain of command**: You formally designate another gem as your second for a session. They gain access to your Starting Move once before the session ends — the directive and the bonus die.
- **[Advanced] Anticipate**: You read a conflict's development well enough to preemptively position. Once per conflict, you may retroactively declare that your gem was somewhere else when an action happened — you saw it coming. The GM determines whether the position is plausible.

## SCHOLAR GEMS — Blue Diamond's Court

### SAPPHIRE
- **Probability collapse**: You identify the exact action that will make a specific outcome impossible — closing a door before it opens. You don't know everything that follows. You know that one door closes. Once per session. The certainty is total. So is the cost of having it.
- **Frozen moment**: You encase a specific object, document, or location in ice that cannot be touched, moved, altered, or destroyed until you release it. Not a weapon — preservation. It can hold something safe for a very long time.
- **[Advanced] Vision share**: You show another gem a specific moment from your future vision in enough detail that they experience it as you did — emotionally, not just informationally. What they do with it remains entirely their choice. You cannot revise what you show.

### LAPIS LAZULI
- **Tidal force**: Your hydrokinesis extends to weather-scale water manipulation — calling rain, generating fog, draining moisture from an area. Takes time and significant emotional investment. At high Harmony the effects are precise. At low Harmony they're not.
- **Water memory**: Water you've controlled retains an imprint you can read after the fact — where it's been, what it's touched, what passed through it. An environmental record. The GM determines how far back the memory goes based on what you ask and what would plausibly be there.
- **[Advanced] Absolute tide**: You call all available water in range simultaneously and hold it. Not a weapon — a statement. The scale depends on Form and current Harmony. This is never subtle. Everyone in the scene understands what's happening.

### AQUAMARINE
- **Fast strike**: Your flight speed applied to a single attack — you move through a scene so quickly that you effectively arrive from angles that don't exist until you're already there. The attack carries both Finesse and Reach simultaneously. Once per conflict.
- **Containment circuit**: Rather than targeting a gem with your retrieval construct, you orbit them — the construct creating a perimeter. They can't leave the orbit without triggering it. You have to keep moving.
- **[Advanced] Swarm retrieval**: Your construct replicates into multiple simultaneous capture attempts on different targets. Each is weaker than a single focused construct. Roll once; the GM distributes partial results across targets.

### ZIRCON
- **Precedent**: You find a historical or legal case that technically applies to the current situation and makes your position defensible. Works retroactively — you can invoke it for something that already happened if you haven't left the scene.
- **Formal objection**: You halt an action in progress by invoking procedural authority loudly and precisely. Even gems who don't recognize Homeworld law hesitate. Buys one exchange of time. Works once per scene before gems stop responding to it.
- **[Advanced] Case closed**: You construct an argument so complete that the opposition loses access to their primary objection for the rest of the scene. Not silence — they can still speak. They just can't use that argument anymore. The GM confirms what specifically is foreclosed.

### LABRADORITE
- **Persistent illusion**: Your illusions hold without active concentration — you set them running and they maintain until physically disturbed. Less detailed than sustained ones, but you're free to act elsewhere while they run.
- **Refraction shield**: You bend incoming light-based and energy attacks around yourself, redirecting rather than blocking. Works only on radiant attacks, not physical ones. The redirected energy goes somewhere — you choose where, within the scene.
- **[Advanced] Total environment**: You construct a complete illusory environment over an existing space — not one false object but everything. Gems inside experience it as real in every sensory register available to them. Requires full concentration. Breaks immediately if you're hit.

### IOLITE
- **Spectrum blindness**: You can suppress your trichroic vision and present as gem-blind across all three spectra in ways other gems can detect and verify. Useful for infiltration. Something about a gem with full sight choosing not to use it makes others uncomfortable in ways they can't articulate.
- **Thermal mapping**: Your infrared vision allows complete environmental mapping of a space — hidden gems, recent movement, heat trails, occupied rooms behind walls. Thorough mapping takes a full scene. Quick mapping tells you one specific thing.
- **[Advanced] Spectral analysis**: Your ultraviolet vision extends to reading the residual signature of gem powers used in a location — what was used, approximately when, and what gem type produced it. Works up to a week back in a closed environment.

### NEPHRITE
- **Warp calculation**: From a fixed position you can identify every warp pad within a planetary system, whether each is active or dormant, and whether any are guarded or compromised. Takes a scene of focused attention.
- **Spatial presence**: Your navigation awareness extends to tactical positioning — you always know where every gem in a conflict is, even those you can't see, as long as you've had a moment to orient in the space.
- **[Advanced] Warp intercept**: You can calculate where a gem went if you reach the pad they used before the signature dissipates. On a clean Clarity roll, the GM tells you the destination precisely. On a partial, approximate. On a miss, you know they went somewhere but not where.

## DIPLOMAT GEMS — Pink Diamond's Court

### ROSE QUARTZ
- **Deep heal**: Extended contact healing that partially addresses a cracked gem's underlying fracture — not just closing surface damage, but working at the structural level. Requires an entire scene and costs significant Form. The GM determines how much of the crack is addressed.
- **Shared stability**: You transfer a portion of your own Form stability to another gem as a buffer. They absorb incoming Form damage; you feel the same cost simultaneously. Entirely voluntary. Cannot be used on yourself.
- **[Advanced] Heal the past**: A single use per campaign (not per session): you repair a cracked gem enough to restore one stat point that was lost to the crack. The time, the scene, and the cost are significant. It is not reversible.

### EMERALD
- **Commandeer**: You formally requisition a resource — ship, equipment, location, vehicle — with enough apparent Homeworld authority that current holders hand it over. Works once before the paperwork catches up and questions are asked.
- **Shipping manifest**: You place gems or objects into Homeworld's shipping network, routed to destinations you specify, under cover of routine logistics. Getting them out at the destination is a separate problem you haven't solved yet.
- **[Advanced] Full authorization**: Once per campaign you invoke a Homeworld authorization so complete and so precisely constructed that it passes thorough investigation. Not indefinitely — the lie has a lifespan — but it holds for long enough.

### MORGANITE
- **Recall**: You reverse a previous assignment or reassignment — yours or another gem's. Complicated. Takes time. Creates a paper trail that someone will eventually follow.
- **Welfare assessment**: You initiate a formal welfare review that makes it bureaucratically necessary for other gems to treat the subject with specific care for a defined period. Interrupting a welfare review has paperwork consequences. Gems who understand institutions know this.
- **[Advanced] Restructure**: You redesign the formal assignment structure of a small team or unit — their roles, their reporting relationships, their recorded functions. The changes are real in Homeworld's systems until someone senior enough notices.

### KUNZITE
- **Emotional anchor**: You provide a gem in crisis with a stable focal point — your own emotional state, steady and legible. They can treat your Resolve as a supplementary stat for a scene, adding it to their rolls where emotional steadiness matters.
- **Read the room (expanded)**: You read the emotional architecture of a gathering rather than an individual — what's suppressed collectively, what's close to the surface, what everyone is performing at once versus what's underneath. Not individuals. The field.
- **[Advanced] Dissipate**: You identify a specific emotional state that's driving a situation — the fear, the grief, the anger — and create enough space around it that it loses its grip on the scene. Not resolved. Set down for now. The GM determines how long "for now" lasts.

### TOURMALINE
- **Spectrum split**: You simultaneously present as two different gem types to different observers in the same scene. Requires active concentration and a Radiance roll; if it slips, both readings collapse simultaneously and everyone in the scene knows something was wrong.
- **Register shift**: You abruptly change your social presentation mid-interaction — the gem you're speaking with must reorient completely. Whatever authority or familiarity they were operating from has to be rebuilt. Their next social action is made with one fewer die.
- **[Advanced] Full spectrum**: Your presentation becomes genuinely unreadable — no gem type reads clearly, no court affiliation registers, no caste signal lands consistently. You are whoever the scene needs and no one can pin down why they believed it. Lasts one scene. Requires a Radiance roll to maintain under direct scrutiny.

### TURQUOISE
- **Historical precedent (extended)**: You invoke a law, tradition, or agreement that predates current Diamond authority and technically supersedes current policy. The GM determines how much weight it carries in this specific context. You know before you use it whether it will hold.
- **Witnessed**: You share something you personally observed from before current Homeworld history — something that predates most living gems' experience of the empire. Gems who hear it must roll Resolve to dismiss what it implies. You have carried this for a very long time.
- **[Advanced] Survivor's knowledge**: Your long existence means you have personally encountered situations structurally identical to this one before. Once per session you may declare this and ask the GM what happened the last time — what worked, what failed, and what the cost was.

## CROSS-COURT GEMS

### PEARL
- **Perfect record**: You project a holographic reconstruction of any event you personally witnessed with enough precision and detail to serve as evidence, testimony, or analytical material. You cannot fabricate. The record is exactly what happened.
- **Training sequence**: You compress the teaching of a physical or technical skill into one scene of focused instruction. The student gains temporary competence — a single bonus die, once — rather than permanent absorption. You know the difference between knowing something and having it in your body.
- **[Advanced] Complete archive**: You have been recording everything since your emergence. Once per campaign you can produce a holographic record of something that happened before you were assigned to anyone — from your earliest formation. The GM determines what you witnessed before your memory was considered relevant to anyone.

### PERIDOT
- **Remote operation**: You operate Homeworld technology at a distance through ferrokinesis — no physical contact required. Range is limited; precision decreases with distance. Non-Homeworld technology at range requires a Clarity roll and significant effort.
- **Improvised weapon**: You construct a functional weapon from available materials in the environment. Choose two tags. It works for one conflict, then falls apart. The GM may rule certain materials unavailable.
- **[Advanced] System override**: You connect to a Homeworld system and take complete control — not just reading it, but rewriting its current operational state. Lasts until someone with appropriate authorization manually reverts it. Requires physical contact and a full scene.

### JADE
- **Immovable**: Once per conflict you simply refuse to move. No roll — you are where you are and you stay there. Anything that would physically relocate you fails. You feel the attempt completely. It doesn't work.
- **Patient read**: Your long practice of observing from positions of low power gives you acute reading ability without the risk of intrusion. Given one scene of observation, you gain a complete Clarity read on a gem's motivations without rolling and without them knowing.
- **[Advanced] Institutional memory**: You have occupied liminal positions across enough courts to have seen nearly everything from the outside. Once per session you may name a social dynamic, power structure, or interpersonal pattern and the GM confirms whether you've seen it before — and what happened.

### CITRINE
- **Role shift**: You adopt a functional role completely for a scene, temporarily accessing the instincts that go with it. A Citrine shifting to Scholar gains Read the Room. Soldier gains First Strike. Diplomat gains Open Channel. Leader gains Hold the Line. Once per session. The GM may expand options based on your history.
- **Versatile cover**: Your comfort across roles makes you genuinely difficult to classify. Gems attempting to assess, read, or evaluate you find their results ambiguous. This doesn't prevent reads — it complicates them.
- **[Advanced] Institutional fit**: You have occupied enough roles that you can step into any organizational structure and appear to belong within three exchanges. You don't have the authorization of the role. You have the bearing, vocabulary, and behavioral signature of someone who does.

## DESIGN NOTES FOR THE GM

**On the [Advanced] tier**: These powers represent either genuinely rare capabilities, ethically significant ones, or things that reshape the fiction in lasting ways. They should cost meaningfully more XP than standard unlocks and in some cases may require a story justification before they become available — not as a gate, but because the power implies something about the gem's history.

**On powers that don't add dice**: Several of these (Immovable, Formal Objection, Witnessed, etc.) work narratively rather than mechanically. This is intentional and consistent with the rulebook's design — weapon tags don't add dice, they determine what's *possible*. These powers work the same way.

**On powers that echo other gem types' abilities**: A few advanced powers deliberately give gems access to something usually associated with another type (Citrine's Role Shift, Hessonite's Chain of Command). This is consistent with the game's theme of exceeding assigned function.

**On the Optional Zero and these powers**: A gem with a stat at Optional Zero cannot use any power that requires a roll in that stat. They can still possess the power — the capability is there in potential — but it cannot function until the stat advances past 0.
